Motion Chart Demo

Category Performance by # of Options

Viz Type: Interactive Motion Chart
Data: Dummy Data
Description: A chart analyzing how the number of options available in a category can impact deal performance. Each bubble represents a deal category, the x-axis is the number of options available, the y-axis is average performance, and the size of the bubble is net units. Press the play button at the bottom of the chart to see the changes over time. Diagonal movement (up and left, or down and right) suggests a direct correlation between # of options and avg performance. Select a bubble to add a trail and track categories individually, or change the axis labels to see other interactions. Change the x-axis to "Time" to see an interactive animated line chart.
Visualization Justification: Motion charts - made famous by Hans Rosling - are essentially animated bubble charts that excel at showing high level patterns as well as the individual elements that make up the pattern. Compared to other visualizations, motion charts can show more dimensions of data simultaneously to tell a better story with data. Alternatively, a motion chart can highlight a lack of correlation between two or more factors.
